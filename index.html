About


I am currently a senior at Mount Holyoke College. I am majoring in Computer Science with a minor in Anthropology. I have taken courses in Data Structures, Systems Engineering, Discrete Mathematics, Linear Algebra, Algorithms, Distributed Systems Engineering, Operating Systems, Gödel's Theorems, and Compiler Design. For my Anthropology minor, I have focused on cultrual anthropology topics.



In the summer of 2019, I collaborated with other interns in the M365 division of Microsoft to devlop a feature concept for Windows 10X. We presented our work to organizational leadership and I partnered with other teams to explore the technical feasibility of contributions to the feature concept.

In summer 2018, I was a Microsoft Garage developer intern. The Garage executes experimental projects from other Microsoft teams. My team completed a redesign of a popular Windows Ink application by implementing new user-requested features. I orchestrated deployment of the redesigned application and coordinated agile scrum development by managing the product backlog, organizing sprint planning, and leading team sprint retrospectives.

In the summer of 2017, I was a research assistant in Heather Pon-Barry's Interactive Computing Research Lab at Mount Holyoke College. Under the NSF National Robotics Initiative, the ICRL is developing a learning companion that can increase STEM engagement for underrepresented minorities through increasing their domain learning and confidence in their skillsets. I developed a remote control user interface for a NAO robot and assisted in conducting a wizard-of-oz style experiment. I additionally conducted research on dyadic stance in human-robot interaction to improve the sociability of the NAO robot. This work eventually led to a paper published in the companion of the 2018 ACM/IEEE International Conference on Human-Robot Interaction.
